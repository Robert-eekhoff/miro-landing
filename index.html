Our story

Why I built Miiro

Miiro comes from the Japanese word miru (見る), meaning to see.

I built it because my wife Mirrin and I kept dropping the ball on the little things. The groceries nobody bought. The kindergarten pickup we both showed up to. The "I thought you were handling that" moments.

The mental load wasn't shared — it was invisible. Every task app out there was built for one person working alone. We needed something that let us see each other's day.

The name is personal: Mi (from Mirrin) + Ro (from Robert) = Miiro.
